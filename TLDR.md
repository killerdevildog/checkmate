# Operation Checkmate TLDR

Operation Checkmate is a proposed Utah statute that makes government actors personally accountable when they abuse public power.

The short version:

**If a private citizen would face consequences for doing it, a government actor should not escape consequences because they had a badge, robe, office, contract, agency title, or government policy behind them.**

This file is the short guide. The full bill is [`bill_proposal.md`](bill_proposal.md). The latest PDF snapshot is [Draft Ver 5.8.5](https://github.com/killerdevildog/checkmate/releases/tag/v5.8.5).

---

## One Sentence

Operation Checkmate gives citizens a way to investigate, expose, prosecute, and sue government actors who use public power unlawfully, dishonestly, coercively, incompetently, or retaliatorily.

---

## The Problem

Right now, many officials can harm citizens and avoid real accountability because of doctrines like:

- Qualified immunity
- Judicial immunity
- Prosecutorial immunity
- Sovereign immunity
- Governmental immunity
- "Discretion"
- "Policy"
- "We were just following procedure"

That means a citizen may lose liberty, property, children, housing, employment, records, benefits, or even life itself, while the official responsible faces no personal criminal or civil consequence.

Operation Checkmate says that gap is unacceptable.

---

## The Core Fix

The bill creates a legal rule:

**Public power is held in trust. When a government actor abuses it, they are personally accountable.**

That means:

- A judge is not immune for unlawful judicial conduct
- A prosecutor is not immune for hiding evidence or prosecuting someone they should know is innocent
- An officer is not immune for unlawful force, intimidation, retaliation, unlawful search, or rights violations
- A clerk, agency worker, jail official, school official, inspector, contractor, or elected official is not immune for abusing public authority
- A government body can be treated as a government actor when the body itself violates the Act

The bill does not target lawful public service. It targets misconduct.

---

## Who It Covers

The bill covers any **government actor**, including:

- Police officers, deputies, jail staff, corrections officers, federal agents, and custody contractors
- Judges, prosecutors, clerks, court staff, probation officers, and parole officers
- Legislators, governors, mayors, council members, commissioners, agency heads, and regulators
- School officials, child-welfare workers, benefits workers, licensing officials, code inspectors, and public-record custodians
- Private contractors or vendors exercising delegated government power
- Government bodies and collective boards when they act through public authority
- Jurors when exercising state power to convict, acquit, indict, or decide guilt

The key idea is not the job title. The key question is whether the person or entity is exercising, invoking, or pretending to exercise state power.

---

## Citizen Councils

The biggest enforcement mechanism is the **Citizen Council**.

Twelve or more citizens can form a Council outside the judicial process. They do not need a judge to convene them. They do not need permission from a prosecutor, agency, court, mayor, sheriff, or political office.

A Citizen Council can:

- Investigate crimes by government actors
- Issue a **Declaration of Prosecution**
- Demand government records through a **Declaration of Production of Records**
- Seek a judge-signed warrant through a **Declaration of Warrant** when private-capacity searches are needed
- Seek an arrest warrant through a **Declaration of Arrest**
- Force the appropriate prosecuting office to act, subject to the procedures in the bill
- Trigger review of logically inconsistent laws
- Review convictions through an 18-member conviction-review process

Interfering with a Citizen Council is itself a serious offense.

---

## The Four Council Declarations

### 1. Declaration of Prosecution

The Council declares that a government actor should be prosecuted. This activates the prosecution pathway in Section 9.

### 2. Declaration of Production of Records

The Council demands records from a government entity. Government entities do not get to use the Fourth Amendment as a shield against public accountability for official-capacity records.

### 3. Declaration of Warrant

If the Council needs to search a public official's private-capacity home, private papers, private devices, or other private effects, the Fourth Amendment applies. A judge reviews it under the same low standard used today for law-enforcement warrant applications.

### 4. Declaration of Arrest

If arrest is needed, a judge reviews the Council's declaration under the same standard used today for police arrest-warrant submissions.

## Rights Protected

The bill protects constitutional and statutory rights broadly, including:

- Due process
- Equal protection
- Freedom of speech, religion, press, assembly, and petition
- The right to record government actors
- The right to counsel
- The right to remain silent
- The right against unlawful search and seizure
- The right to parent and maintain family integrity
- The right to access courts, records, and government process
- The right not to be punished for poverty, homelessness, disability, language barriers, or inability to pay
- The right not to be retaliated against for asserting rights

When there is ambiguity, the bill tells readers to construe protected rights in favor of the citizen and against the government actor.

---

## No Immunity for Unlawful Power

Section 5 is the accountability backbone.

It ends immunity defenses within the scope of the Act for unlawful conduct, including:

- Qualified immunity
- Judicial immunity
- Prosecutorial immunity
- Sovereign immunity
- Legislative immunity
- Executive immunity
- Analogous doctrines of absolute or partial immunity

The rule is simple:

**Immunity, if recognized at all, protects lawful governance. It does not transform unlawful conduct into lawful conduct.**

---

## Private Capacity vs Official Capacity

The bill separates two ideas:

- When a government actor acts as a private citizen, they keep ordinary private-citizen rights.
- When a government actor acts in official capacity or under color of authority, they are the state for that conduct.

That means officials cannot use personal free speech, privacy, personal opinion, or private rights as a defense for official conduct that violates a citizen's rights.

The badge, robe, title, office, or agency power constrains the actor. It does not expand the actor's rights.

---

## Mental States

The bill uses four culpability levels:

- **Negligent**: the actor failed to perceive a risk they should have perceived
- **Reckless**: the actor consciously disregarded a risk or ignored an obvious risk
- **Intentional**: the actor acted on purpose or with awareness the result was substantially certain
- **Malicious**: the actor acted intentionally with ill will, retaliation, concealment, intimidation, or desire to harm

Recklessness is usually the criminal baseline unless a specific section says otherwise.

---

## New or Important Offenses

The bill names many kinds of official misconduct. Some of the most important:

- **Assault by a government actor**: creating reasonable fear that a citizen must defend their rights
- **Battery by a government actor**: directly depriving liberty, property, family, bodily integrity, or rights
- **Cabalism**: coordinated small-group abuse of government power
- **Government racketeering**: patterns of official corruption or coercion
- **Obstruction of justice**: failure or refusal to perform official duties in a way that destroys justice
- **Treason**: war against citizens, the Constitution, or the state in the Act's defined sense
- **Anarchy**: a government actor acting completely outside lawful authority
- **Terrorism by government actor**: official terror, threats, intimidation, or coercion
- **Official intimidation**: official conduct causing undue fear or distress
- **Malicious prosecution**: continuing prosecution after knowing or recklessly disregarding that the citizen is innocent
- **Failure to serve**: failure to perform an affirmative public duty
- **Incompetence in office**: holding or exercising public authority while recklessly disregarding a substantial possibility of incompetence for the role

Incompetence in office scales from Class 1 misdemeanor to capital offense depending on the harm caused, including confusion, deprivation, conviction, suicide, death, or equivalent permanent liberty loss.

---

## Hearsay and Statement Evidence

The bill now gives hearsay its own top-level section.

The core rule is equal use: if a statement can be used against a citizen, the same kind of statement can be used by the citizen as a defense.

The bill treats *"I heard him say..."* as a fact the witness can testify to. The trier of fact can still decide whether the witness is credible, whether the statement was made, what it meant, and how much weight it deserves.

The point is not that every statement is automatically true. The point is that all testimony is a human representation of truth, not truth itself. Hearsay cannot be used as a one-way weapon for prosecution while being blocked when it helps the defense.

One-way hearsay suppression becomes its own offense and can escalate where it affects charges, pleas, convictions, custody, family separation, or death.

---

## Courts and Prosecutors

The bill creates affirmative duties for prosecutors and judges.

Prosecutors must:

- Disclose adverse evidence
- Stop prosecuting when the evidence shows the accused is innocent
- Seek reduction or dismissal when the charge no longer fits the facts
- Not use prosecutorial discretion as a shield for corruption or selective protection

Judges must:

- Recuse when required
- Engage evidence and law on the merits
- Avoid ex parte communications
- Apply mandatory rules correctly
- Correct errors when discovered
- Not use contempt threats, silence, delay, or rubber-stamping to crush rights

The point is that due process must be real, not ceremonial.

---

## Juries

The bill strengthens jury due process.

Jurors can ask questions. They cannot be removed just for independent reasoning or disagreement with the judge or prosecutor.

Convicting jurors must document their reasoning on evidence so a wrongfully convicted person has something real to appeal.

Verdict options include:

- Convict
- Convict with refinement
- Convict and forgive
- Innocent
- Unequivocally innocent
- Acquit without stated reason

---

## Inmates Are Included

Every inmate in a federal, state, county, municipal, detention, jail, holding, or correctional facility must receive a printed copy of the statute.

Inmates may also form Citizen Councils.

That matters because people in custody are often the people most directly controlled by government power and most vulnerable to retaliation.

The bill protects inmate council activity, communication, filings, and access to materials.

---

## Retroactive Civil Claims and No Time Bar

Civil claims can be brought for prior conduct that would violate the Act, even if the conduct occurred before the Act's effective date.

Criminal penalties apply prospectively.

The bill also states there is no statute of limitations for civil or criminal actions under the title.

It further treats official excuse-making, gaslighting, post-hoc rationalization, or logically inconsistent application of law or fact as prima facie evidence of guilt or civil liability in proceedings under the Act.

---

## Penalty Enhancements

Penalties can increase when:

- The victim was detained, restrained, or unable to leave
- The act was committed under pretense of lawful authority
- Multiple actors participated
- Rights were deprived for a long time
- The violation caused suicidal ideation
- The violation caused extreme fear, anxiety, or distress
- The violation caused symptoms of psychological harm supported by qualified evaluation

If a violation leads to suicide, Section 12 applies.

---

## Repugnant Government Instruments

Section 16 says government instruments that conflict with the Act or constitutional authority are null and void.

That can include:

- Statutes
- Ordinances
- Court orders
- Warrants
- Judgments
- Regulations
- Agency policies
- Internal practices
- Memos
- Subpoenas
- Administrative actions

If the instrument is repugnant, citizens are not liable for refusing to comply with it. Government actors who enforce it may be criminally liable.

---

## Logical Consistency Review

A unanimous 12-member Citizen Council can require review of a statute, regulation, ordinance, court rule, or other legal rule that is logically inconsistent with:

- The U.S. Constitution
- The Utah Constitution
- This Act
- Another statute or rule still in force

This is meant to force government to answer contradictions instead of hiding behind habit, jargon, or "that is just how it works."

---

## Conviction Review

A unanimous 18-member Citizen Council can review a criminal conviction in Utah.

The Council can:

- Nullify a conviction
- Reduce a conviction
- Spare a person from death-penalty consequences
- Extend public forgiveness
- Restore civil and political rights
- Bar collateral consequences where the bill allows

This creates a citizen-facing remedy for convictions the ordinary appeal system failed to fix.

---

## Forfeiture of Benefits and Office

Government actors found liable under the Act can lose:

- Public office
- State-conferred benefits
- Pension or retirement benefits tied to the office
- Eligibility for future positions of state power
- State contracts or state-derived benefits where applicable

The bill also bars using government funds, insurance, or public resources to indemnify individual government actors found liable under the Act.

## Examples

### Traffic Stop Without Basis

An officer stops a citizen without articulable lawful basis. Under the bill, this may be Anarchy, Battery against rights, Enforcement Without Articulable Basis, or another role-specific offense depending on the facts.

### Prosecutor Hides Evidence

A prosecutor has evidence undermining the charge but does not disclose it. Under the bill, this can be obstruction of justice and may support malicious prosecution if the case continues anyway.

### Judge Rubber-Stamps a Warrant

A judge signs a warrant without meaningful review. Under the bill, the judge may violate the duty to engage on the merits, and the warrant itself may be repugnant if unsupported by lawful authority.

### Clerk Blocks a Filing

A clerk refuses, delays, misroutes, or buries a citizen filing without lawful written authority. Under the bill, this can be clerk access obstruction and possibly obstruction of justice.

### Jail Denies Medication

A jail official denies necessary medication, medical care, mental-health care, suicide prevention, hygiene, food, water, or protection from known violence. Under the bill, this can be custodial medical abandonment and may escalate if serious harm or death results.

### Official Uses "Policy" but Will Not Identify It

An official says "policy requires it" but refuses to identify the policy, source, or lawful authority. Under the bill, this can be a Policy Shield Statement offense.

### Citizen Records Government

A citizen records police, court, school, agency, jail, or public-meeting activity. A government actor threatens, blocks, detains, cites, seizes a device, deletes data, or retaliates because of the recording. Under the bill, that can be Recording-Right Interference.

### Official Is Incompetent for the Role

A government actor continues exercising authority after warnings, reversals, failures, or obvious facts show a substantial possibility that the actor is not competent for the role. Under the bill, that can be Incompetence in Office. If the incompetence causes confusion, charges, conviction, incarceration, suicide, death, or permanent liberty loss, the offense escalates.

---

## What This Bill Is Not

Operation Checkmate is not:

- A license for citizens to ignore valid law
- A sovereign-citizen theory
- An anti-police or anti-judge revenge bill
- A replacement for ordinary criminal law
- A guarantee that every complaint becomes a conviction
- Legal advice

It is a proposed accountability statute.

It says lawful public service remains lawful, but unlawful public power has consequences.

---

## Why Supporters Think It Matters

Supporters believe:

- Rights without enforcement are decorations
- Immunity has become a shield for abuse
- Prosecutors should not be the only gatekeepers for prosecuting government actors
- Citizens need a lawful, peaceful mechanism to force accountability
- Courts should answer contradictions, not hide behind procedure
- Inmates, poor citizens, parents, defendants, workers, protesters, and ordinary people need real access to justice
- Public trust improves when public servants are actually accountable

Hi [name],

I wanted to share a few thoughts from the Book of Mormon that seem deeply relevant to the idea that public officials, judges, lawyers, and government leaders should be held to a higher standard and remain accountable under the law.

In **Alma 10**, Amulek warns the people of Ammonihah that corruption among their lawyers and judges was laying the foundation for their destruction. He says, "the foundation of the destruction of this people is beginning to be laid by the unrighteousness of your lawyers and your judges." That is a powerful warning. The law was meant to protect truth and justice, not to shield the powerful from accountability.

In **Alma 60**, Captain Moroni also condemns government leaders who neglect their duty while innocent people suffer. He writes, "Can you think to sit upon your thrones in a state of thoughtless stupor, while your enemies are spreading the work of death around you?" He then calls upon the government to repent and fulfill its duty to protect the people. That shows that leaders are not exempt from moral judgment. In fact, because they hold power, they carry greater responsibility before God and the people.

The Book of Mormon also gives a serious warning about the **Gadianton robbers**. In **Helaman 6**, they operated through secret oaths, hidden signs, murder, plunder, and mutual protection. Their purpose was to gain power and wealth while avoiding punishment. The scripture says they had "their signs, yea, their secret signs, and their secret words," so they could recognize and protect one another. It also says they sought to "murder, and to plunder, and to steal, and to commit whoredoms and all manner of wickedness, contrary to the laws of their country and also the laws of their God."

What made the Gadianton robbers so dangerous was not only their violence, but their infiltration of society and government. **Helaman 6** says they eventually obtained "the sole management of the government." That is one of the clearest warnings in scripture about corrupt networks gaining power inside public institutions.

Moroni gives the same warning in **Ether 8**. He explains that secret combinations are built to "get power and gain," and he warns future readers that when they see these things among them, they must "awake to a sense of your awful situation." He also says these combinations seek to "overthrow the freedom of all lands, nations, and countries." That warning applies whenever secrecy, intimidation, violence, deception, or unlawful influence is used to gain power over a people.

The Book of Mormon also teaches that secret works are meant to be exposed. In **Alma 37**, Alma says God will bring "all their secret works and their abominations" out of darkness and into light. That is how modern Gadianton-style corruption is defeated: not by fear, silence, or blind loyalty, but by truth, lawful accountability, repentance, and courage.

So when we talk about corruption, secret combinations, or terroristic conduct within government, I believe the Book of Mormon invites us to ask serious questions: Are leaders protecting the innocent? Are laws being applied equally? Are public officials accountable? Are hidden works being brought to light? Are people seeking power and gain at the expense of freedom?

To me, the Book of Mormon is clear: no one is above the law, especially those entrusted with power.

That is the objective of **Operation Checkmate**: to stop government corruption absolutely, expose secret combinations, bring hidden works to light, and ensure that no official, agency, or hidden network stands above the law.

Hi [name],

I wanted to send you something I have been working on called **Operation Checkmate**.

The basic idea is simple: citizens are disciplined by criminal law, and the military is disciplined by the **Uniform Code of Military Justice (UCMJ)**. But ordinary government power still does not have an equivalent criminal discipline when officials misuse public authority against citizens.

That is the gap this bill is trying to close.

When a citizen violates the law, the criminal code reaches them. When a soldier violates military duty, the UCMJ reaches them. But when a judge, prosecutor, officer, agency actor, regulator, elected official, or other government actor violates the rights of a citizen under color of authority, the system often hides behind immunity doctrines, discretion, procedure, and institutional self-protection.

Operation Checkmate says that should end.

There are no kings in America. A badge, robe, office, title, commission, or election does not turn a person into someone above the law. Public power is supposed to be limited, defined, and held in trust for the people. When that power is used to assault, batter, obstruct, intimidate, retaliate against, or deprive the rights of a citizen, there should be enforceable criminal accountability.

The missing piece in our country is a disciplined government. Operation Checkmate is an attempt to write that discipline clearly: no immunity for rights violations, no special escape hatch for public office, and a citizen-led enforcement mechanism when existing gatekeepers refuse to act.

Please take a look. If you support it, the instructions for signing on are at the bottom of the bill.

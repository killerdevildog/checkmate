Hey [name],

I've been drafting a piece of legislation called **Operation Checkmate**, and I wanted to share it with you because I think it speaks to something we both care about — accountability, truth, and a society where the people who govern us are answerable to the same standard the rest of us live by.

At its heart, the bill says: every person — no matter what office, badge, robe, or title — is accountable to actual reality and to the law. **There are no kings.**

The current system has stacked legal doctrines that protect judges, prosecutors, and other officials from being held accountable when they harm citizens — even maliciously. The bill names those doctrines by the actual Supreme Court cases that built them up — *Bradley v. Fisher*, *Stump v. Sparkman*, *Mireles v. Waco*, *Imbler v. Pachtman*, *Harlow v. Fitzgerald* — and ends them within the scope of the Act.

It also gives ordinary citizens — not the political class — the means to enforce that accountability, through Citizen Councils that can investigate and force prosecution without depending on the gatekeepers who currently shield misconduct.

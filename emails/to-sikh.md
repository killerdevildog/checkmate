**Subject:** Sikh principles on truthful living and accountable public service

---

Sat Sri Akaal [name],

I wanted to share something I have been working on called **Operation Checkmate**, and I wanted to explain why I believe it connects with Sikh principles of truthful living, seva, equality, and the welfare of all people.

The **Guru Granth Sahib** teaches that truth is high, but truthful living is higher still. That principle matters in public life. It is not enough for officials to speak about justice, law, rights, or service. They must live truthfully in the way they use authority.

Sikh tradition also emphasizes seva, service without selfishness. Public office should be understood in that spirit: service to the people, not a path to personal power, protection, retaliation, or gain. When a government servant uses authority to harm citizens or hide wrongdoing, that is the opposite of seva.

The Sikh prayerful ideal of **sarbat da bhala**, the welfare of all, also points toward public systems that protect everyone, not only the powerful or well-connected. A just system should protect the poor, the ordinary citizen, the falsely accused, the unpopular, and even the person who has no influence.

The Guru Granth Sahib also teaches a vision of human dignity beyond enmity and exclusion: no one is an enemy, and no one is a stranger. That spirit rejects abusive power. It calls for public conduct rooted in humility, truth, justice, and respect for the dignity of all.

To me, Operation Checkmate is built around those principles. It is not anti-government. It is anti-corruption. It says that people with public power should be accountable under law when they violate rights, abuse authority, obstruct justice, retaliate against citizens, or protect hidden wrongdoing.

That is the objective of **Operation Checkmate**: to stop government corruption absolutely, expose hidden abuse of public power, protect citizens from official misconduct, and ensure that no official, agency, court, or hidden network stands above the law.

Sincerely,

[Your name]

The operative reach is structurally novel; the operative reasoning that produces the reach is structurally direct. The operative principle: government exists to secure the citizen's pre-existing rights ([why_does_the_government_even_exist.md](why_does_the_government_even_exist.md)); where the government's response to a citizen in operative crisis is the carceral process rather than the operative help the constitutional architecture contemplates, the operative response inverts the operative purpose of government, and the operative inversion is itself reachable as a substantive offense.

## 1. The Operative Foundation — Government Exists to Secure Rights, Not to Criminalize Their Absence

The Founders' framework, developed in [why_does_the_government_even_exist.md](why_does_the_government_even_exist.md), treats government as the citizen's agent, instituted "to secure these rights" (Declaration of Independence). The operative purpose is the operative protection of the citizen's life, liberty, and the operative material conditions under which the citizen can sustain operative life. §1.1.47 addresses the operative pattern in which government, instead of addressing the operative conditions, responds to the citizen's operative distress through the operative carceral apparatus — arrests, citations, criminal charges, detention, removal of property, incarceration. The operative pattern inverts the operative purpose of government. The provision reaches the operative inversion as a substantive offense.

### 1.1 The Operative Inversion

The operative inversion can be stated directly. The constitutional architecture treats government as the operative resource available to the citizen in operative distress. The operative practice has, across the operative arc of modern American policy, increasingly treated the citizen in operative distress as the operative problem the government's carceral apparatus is deployed against. The operative effect is the operative criminalization of survival conduct — sleeping, eating, urinating, gathering, holding belongings, asking for help, existing in public — performed by citizens whose operative conditions leave them no operative alternative to performing the survival conduct in the operative places and operative manners that the operative criminal architecture reaches.

The operative inversion is documented across the operative arc of modern American policy. Empirical research by the National Law Center on Homelessness and Poverty, the National Homelessness Law Center, the Department of Justice's own *Investigation of the Ferguson Police Department* (2015), and the broader operative academic and journalistic record has documented the operative pattern at every operative level — federal, state, county, municipal. The operative pattern is not isolated; it is the operative norm in operative jurisdictions across the United States.

### 1.2 The Operative Principle the Inversion Violates

The operative principle the inversion violates is foundational. Locke's *Second Treatise of Government* § 123 frames the operative purpose of political society as "the mutual preservation of their lives, liberties and estates." The citizen joins political society for the operative preservation; the operative consequence is that political society exists to perform the operative preservation. A political society that, in its operative practice, criminalizes the operative conditions the operative preservation was designed to address has departed from the operative purpose the citizen joined the operative society to advance.

The companion document [why_we_are_a_christian_nation.md](why_we_are_a_christian_nation.md) develops the operative parallel from the etymological framework of Christian disposition. The operative root *charizomai* — granting freely, showing favor, forgiving, bestowing in kindness — operates in operative tension with the operative pattern §1.1.47 reaches. Where government's operative response to a citizen in operative distress is *charizomai*, the operative disposition fits the operative purpose the constitutional architecture contemplates. Where the operative response is the carceral apparatus, the operative disposition is the operative opposite — extraction, coercion, the operative criminalization of the operative conditions the operative response was designed to address.

§1.1.47 names the operative pattern and reaches it as a substantive offense. The operative provision is the operative codification of the operative principle the constitutional and Christian-dispositional architectures jointly support.

## 2. The Pre-Charging Crisis-Inquiry Duty

The operative core of §1.1.47 is the pre-charging crisis-inquiry duty. The duty operates as the operative architecture of legitimate government response to a citizen in operative distress. The operative duty:

- **Is non-delegable.** The operative actor performing the operative encounter with the citizen — the officer, the inspector, the regulator, the agency employee, the prosecutor at charging — performs the operative duty personally and cannot operatively transfer the operative duty to an operative process, intermediary, or downstream actor.
- **Operates before the operative charging decision.** The operative duty is performed at the operative point of encounter, not after the operative criminal process has been initiated. - **Includes operative inquiry into the operative conditions producing the operative conduct.** The operative inquiry tests whether the citizen's operative conduct — the survival conduct, the distress conduct, the operative behavior the operative apparatus would otherwise treat as the operative basis for carceral response — arises from operative conditions in the trigger list (Section 3 below).
- **Includes operative resolution where the operative inquiry produces the operative finding.** Where the operative conduct arises from the operative trigger conditions, the operative actor's duty is to operatively address the operative conditions through the operative resources the government has the operative means to deploy, not to operatively criminalize the operative conduct.

### 2.1 The Operative Structure of the Duty

The duty is structured to operate at the operative point of encounter. The operative test is direct: at the operative moment the operative actor encounters the citizen, does the operative actor have operative reason to believe the citizen's operative conduct arises from one or more of the operative trigger conditions? Where the operative answer is yes, the operative duty attaches; the operative actor must operatively inquire into the operative conditions and operatively address them through the operative resources the government has the operative means to deploy.

The operative duty does not require the operative actor to be a social worker, a medical professional, or a housing administrator. The operative duty requires the operative actor to perform the operative inquiry — to recognize the operative conditions where they are operatively present — and to operatively connect the citizen with the operative resources that exist in the operative jurisdiction. The operative inquiry is calibrated to the operative competence of the operative actor; the operative resolution is calibrated to the operative resources available.

### 2.2 The Operative Effect of the Duty

Where the duty is operatively performed, the operative response to the citizen's distress is the operative help the operative conditions require. Where the duty is not performed and the operative actor proceeds to operatively deploy the carceral apparatus against a citizen whose operative conduct arose from operative trigger conditions, the operative actor has committed the operative offense the section reaches. The operative duty operates as the operative gate between the operative help the constitutional architecture contemplates and the operative carceral response §1.1.47 prohibits.

### 2.3 The Operative Defense Architecture

§1.1.47 includes operative defense architecture calibrated to the operative reality of operative encounters. An operative actor who, in operative good faith, performs the operative inquiry on a developed operative record, applies operative reasonable judgment to the operative facts, and proceeds to operative carceral response only where the operative inquiry has determined that the operative conditions are not operatively present is not reached by §1.1.47.
